Meaning of biography in English
 
biography
noun [ C or U ]
uk  /baɪˈɒɡ.rə.fi/ us  /baɪˈɑː.ɡrə.fi/
Add to word list Add to word list 
B1
the life story of a person written by someone else:
He wrote a biography of Winston Churchill.
Compare
autobiographylife historylife story
 Fewer examples
This biography offers a few glimpses of his life before he became famous.
Her biography revealed that she was not as rich as everyone thought.
The biography was a bit of a rush job.
The biography is an attempt to uncover the inner man.
The biography is woven from the many accounts which exist of things she did.

biography
noun [ C/U ]
us  /bɑɪˈɑɡ·rə·fi/
Add to word list Add to word list 
literature
the story of the life of a person written by someone else, or the area of literature relating to books that describe such stories:
[ C ] He wrote a biography of Lincoln.
biographical
adjective [ not gradable ] us  /ˌbɑɪ·əˈɡræf·ɪ·kəl/
The funeral elegy provides a treasure of biographical information about Shakespeare.
(Definition of biography from the Cambridge Academic Content Dictionary © Cambridge University Press)
